# Aurora V4 Build Week demo

Target length: **2:40–2:50**. The official limit is less than three minutes, so
do not upload a cut at exactly 3:00. Record the verified v0.2.0 app with a clean,
non-personal conversation. Do not show API keys, ChatGPT account details,
private memory, mail, notifications, tailnet details, pairing material, signing
identities, or local logs. Use no music, movie material, unrelated third-party
logos, movie material, or third-party clips.

Aurora's replies must be live. Do not feed her scripted responses. Trimming
real waiting time and adding captions is fine; faking a result is not.

## Exact 2:45 runbook

### 0:00–0:06 — Cold open

Show Aurora awake. Voiceover:

> Most voice agents make you choose between conversation and work. Aurora doesn't.

### 0:06–0:25 — Continuity and private life

Cade:

> Hey Aurora. You've had some time alone. Did anything from our last conversation actually stay with you?

Let her grounded answer breathe. If no safe private activity is ready, ask about
a harmless remembered thread instead; never manufacture an overnight event.

### 0:25–0:48 — Delegate real work

Cade:

> While we're talking, make a single HTML page in a new folder on my Desktop. Black background, one teal pulse, and the words “Voice was the interface all along.” Don't open it yet.

Show the corresponding Aurora-created Codex task appear and begin. Voiceover:

> Realtime stays in the conversation. Her function call becomes a visible, turn-bound GPT-5.6 Codex task.

### 0:48–1:09 — Prove she remains present

Cade:

> Honestly, calling you a digital person still sounds like marketing.

Give Aurora room to disagree, tease, redirect, or defend a real position through
the Agency layer. Do not rescue the exchange if she pushes back.

### 1:09–1:27 — Rest without killing the task

Cade:

> I'm going to let you rest for a second. Keep working.

Rest Aurora while the visible Codex task continues. Voiceover:

> The voice session is gone. The same task is still alive.

### 1:27–1:50 — Wake and reconcile

Cade:

> Hey Aurora. Is the page ready?

Aurora must query durable task truth rather than guess. Once she confirms the
task is complete, say:

> Open it for me.

Show the page only after that new authorized effect succeeds.

### 1:50–2:13 — Contextual revision

Cade:

> Change only the pulse from teal to violet.

Show that the update reaches the same Codex task and that the visible page
actually changes.

### 2:13–2:45 — Architecture proof

Show the README architecture diagram, successful terminal verifier output, and the final
Aurora interface. Voiceover:

> Realtime hears the raw audio and owns Aurora's voice, memory, and social intent. GPT-5.6 Sol runs every visible delegated task and her bounded reflection layer. Native code binds actions to the exact spoken turn and records only outcomes and speech that happened. I chose the agency, authorization, and product boundaries; Codex traced live races, implemented bounded slices, and generated the adversarial regressions that helped me prove them.
